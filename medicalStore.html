MEDHelp

Managing medical inventories, made easy



The Problem:





Context

A medical store management system designed for medical store workers/owners to easily keep track of their medicines and store/edit data about new medicines or patients.

An attempt to challenge the current systems used by pharmacies in India and make a hassle free way to work with medicine data.




Details

Length: 3 months (college project)

Role: User researcher, designer and front end developer

Team : 3 members





Challenges



Research:





User Interviews



  • Interviewed two medical store professionals, each working in a different pharmacy
  • Tried to understand their issues with the existing system, without proposing our new system
  • Noted where they seemed to struggle and found their consumer side, not their complainer side.

Understanding:




Persona

Photo by Stephanie Liverani on Unsplash




Meet Karla, she’s 27, works at MedPlus(a pharmacy) part time and has recently married her husband Jay. She is not very tech savvy and easily annoyed when a computer doesn’t behave the way she expects it to.

Goals:

  • To not take so much effort for her part time job.
  • Easy interactions with customers that don’t end in her getting eye rolled.
  • Go home in a happy mood to her new husband.

Pain points:

  • Not wanting to run to the back of the store every time, to check if a medicine is available
  • Since she works part time, she doesn’t know every regular costumer (customers with long time diseases which require constant medicines). She hates listening to customers explain to her how they’re regulars and don’t feel the need to carry their prescriptions all the time.
  • Finds it cumbersome to go through the log book every time to check for the customer.
